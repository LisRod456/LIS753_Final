Community

On this page, we will discuss various examples of community in Studio Ghibli films.

In Japanese culture, there is a strong sense of community, being a part of something larger than yourself. VS American culture, which is very focused on individualism. Both have positives and negatives, but in Ghibli films, there is a strong sense of "it takes a village."

Sense of Community + Aiding Main Characters

  • Castle in the Sky: Although they start out as antagonists in the film, Dola and her pirate family become friends with Sheeta and Pazu, often going out of their way to protect them.
  • Castle in the Sky: The villagers also help Sheeta and Pazu when they realize that the military are trying to kidnap Sheeta.
  • Kiki's Delivery Service: The baker Fukuo, the baker's wife Osono, the young artist Ursula, and the grandmother all aid Kiki in her journey, reminding her that she is not alone.
  • Whisper of the Heart: Amasawa's grandfather aids Shizuku by offering to be the first person to read her novel and give her feedback on it.
